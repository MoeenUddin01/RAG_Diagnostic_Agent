How It Works

1

Image Upload

Upload a clear photo of your plant leaf showing any visible symptoms or abnormalities.

2

EfficientNet-B2 Analysis

Our deep learning model, built on EfficientNet-B2 architecture, processes your image through convolutional neural networks trained on 13,474 labeled plant disease images across 15 disease classes.

3

Disease Classification

The model identifies the specific disease with high accuracy, providing confidence scores for each prediction across Pepper, Potato, and Tomato crop types.

4

RAG-Powered Insights

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system searches our knowledge base to provide relevant treatment recommendations and management practices for the identified disease.

About Our Model

Architecture

EfficientNet-B2 - A state-of-the-art convolutional neural network optimized for accuracy and efficiency, using compound scaling to uniformly scale depth, width, and resolution.

Training Data

Trained on the PlantVillage dataset with 13,474 images covering 15 distinct disease classes across three major crops: Pepper, Potato, and Tomato.

Two-Phase Training

Warm-up phase with frozen backbone (5 epochs) followed by full fine-tuning (25 epochs) for optimal feature extraction and classification performance.

Class Imbalance Handling

Uses WeightedRandomSampler to ensure balanced training across all classes, preventing bias toward more common diseases.

About PlantDoc

Our Mission

PlantDoc aims to democratize plant disease diagnosis by making AI-powered detection accessible to everyone. We help farmers, gardeners, and agricultural workers identify plant diseases early, reducing crop loss and promoting sustainable agriculture practices.

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), up to 40% of global crop production is lost annually due to plant diseases and pests. Early detection and accurate diagnosis are critical to preventing these losses and ensuring food security.
